# Witter Inventory Movement — Staff Guide

**How to track and move inventory at Webster.**
Open the app on your phone or tablet. Every time a game changes hands or changes
location, you log it here. That's it.

---

## The one rule

> **If a game moves, you log it. Every hand-off, every time.**

The app does the rest — it updates where the game is and what state it's in
automatically. You only ever enter information once.

---

## Getting started (first time)

1. Open the app link (bookmark it / add to home screen).
2. **Pick your name** when prompted. The app remembers you, so every move you log
   is stamped with your name. Pick the right person — it's the custody record.
3. You'll land on the **Move a game** screen. That's home base.

---

## The screens (bottom/side menu)

| Screen | What it's for |
|---|---|

| **Check-in** | Receive inventory arriving at Webster and verify the count. |
| **Dashboard** | See everything at a glance — what's where, and any alerts. |
| **Safes** | See what's inside each safe and whether any safe is over its value cap. |
| **Shows** | Tonight's lineups — which games belong to which show. |
| **Log** | The full history of every movement (the audit trail). |
| **Returns** | Games that came back unsold and need to get back to HQ. |

---

## How to log a move (the core task)

1. Go to **Move**.
2. **Pick the game** (search by game # or title).
   - The app shows you where it is now and its value, so you can confirm you've got the right one.
3. **Tap the movement type** (see the list below). The form changes to ask only
   what that step needs — don't worry about fields that aren't shown.
4. **Fill in what it asks** (destination safe, counts, streamer, etc.).
5. Tap **Log movement**. Done — you'll see a green ✓ confirmation.

## The movement types — what each one means and when to use it

Think of these as the journey of a game, in order:

### 1. Transport — *Lombard → Webster*
The game leaves the Lombard vault headed to Webster.
- **You enter:** the coin count (how many coins are in the case).
- No safe yet — the game is "in transit" until someone checks it in.
- **Who does this:** the mover/driver packing the case.

### 2. Check-in — *Arrived at Webster*
The game shows up at Webster and you receive it into a master safe.
- **You enter:** which master safe, the **actual count** you physically counted, and the **seal status**.
- ⚠️ **If the count doesn't match** what was expected, the app warns you in amber. **Flag it before accepting** — this is the "says 400, only 300 in the box" catch.
- 🚨 **If the seal is broken/tampered**, the app tells you to **photograph it and escalate to management** immediately.
- **Who does this:** Joe, Colton, Rocky, or the coordinator receiving the shipment.

### 3. Stage — *Holding in the master safe*
The game sits in the master safe, ready to be handed out to a streamer.
- **You enter:** which master safe.
- **Who does this:** whoever's managing the master safe.

### 4. Delegate — *Out to a streamer*
You assign the game to a streamer for their show and move it into their room safe.
- **You enter:** which streamer, which show (optional), and their room/senior/junior safe.
- **Who does this:** Joe, Colton, Rocky, or a senior streamer delegating.

### 5. Completed handoff — *Show's done*
The show wrapped. The streamer labeled everything and it's going to booking.
- **You enter:** check the **"labeling double-checked"** box, enter **sold** and **unsold** counts, and the completed/booking drop safe.
- **Who does this:** the streamer closing out, or the point person retrieving the show.

### 6. Booking pull — *Booking takes it*
Booking picks up the completed show from the booking safe.
- **You enter:** who in booking received it.
- **Who does this:** booking team (e.g. Earl).

### 7. Ship — *Into shipping*
Booking finished; it's going to shipping.
- **You enter:** the **ship bucket** (Main holding / In-process / Ready to ship) and a **tracking #** if you have one.
- **Who does this:** shipping team.

### 8. Return — *Unsold or leftover*
Coins that didn't sell, or leftover auction material.
- **You enter:** the **reason** — this routes it correctly:
  - **Unsold → HQ** (goes back to Lombard)
  - **Leftover auction → Auction safe** (stays at Webster for the next auction)
  - **Plan B / didn't run**
- **Who does this:** the streamer or point person handling leftovers.

### 9. Arrive HQ — *Back home, loop closed*
The returned game made it back to Lombard.
- **You enter:** who received it.
- **Who does this:** whoever receives it at Lombard.

## Receiving a shipment (Check-in screen)

When a case arrives from Lombard:

1. Open **Check-in**.
2. Bring the case into the safe room (don't leave it out).
3. For each game/box, **check the seal** — is it intact? If anything looks peeled, sliced, or torn, **photograph it and call management**. Don't accept it quietly.
4. **Count what's actually there** and enter the number. If it doesn't match the expected count, the app flags it — **escalate before you accept it**.
5. Assign it to the right master safe and confirm.

This 30-second check is the most important thing you do — it's a million dollars of coins.

## Reading the Dashboard

The Dashboard is your "is everything okay?" screen. Watch for these alerts:

| Alert | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **⚠ Count mismatch** | A game's actual count ≠ expected. | Find the game, recount, escalate if real. |
| **🚨 High-value discrepancy** | A mismatch on an expensive game. | Escalate to a manager immediately. |
| **⚠ Over cap** | A safe holds more value than it's allowed. | Move some inventory out, or tell a manager. |
| **🚨 Seal compromised** | A broken seal was logged. | Should already be escalated — confirm management knows. |
| **Returns overdue** | An unsold game has been sitting in "Returned" too long without making it back to HQ. | Get it moving back to Lombard and log **Arrive HQ**. |

## Quick tips

- **Always pick the right "you"** at the start — your name is the custody stamp.
- **Log it when it happens**, not at the end of the day. The whole point is real-time visibility.
- **Use Notes** for anything unusual ("seal looked off," "short by 2, recounted, OK").
- **One game per move.** Shows are handed off whole and complete — never split a show between teams or combine shows.
- If the app **warns you (amber/red)**, don't ignore it. Those warnings exist because the mistakes they catch are expensive.
- **Don't see the field you expect?** The form only shows fields for the move type you picked. Pick the right type and the right fields appear.

## If something's wrong

- **Wrong move logged?** Tell a manager — moves are corrected in Airtable, not deleted by staff.
- **App won't load / can't find a game?** Pull-to-refresh or reopen. If a game truly isn't there, it may not be entered yet — check with the inventory team.
- **Seal broken, count short, or anything suspicious?** **Photograph it and escalate to management right away.** When in doubt, escalate.

moment anyone can answer: where is this game, and who touched it last.*
